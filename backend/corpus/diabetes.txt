Diabetes is a chronic, metabolic disease characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose (or blood sugar), which leads over time to serious damage to the heart, blood vessels, eyes, kidneys and nerves.
Type 2 diabetes is the most common, accounting for around 90% of all diabetes cases. It is usually treated with lifestyle changes, such as diet and exercise, and sometimes medication.
There is no complete cure for diabetes, but it can be managed effectively.
